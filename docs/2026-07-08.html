General AI Developments

Beijing is looking at curbing overseas access to China's top AI models
The Reuters story claiming China will restrict foreign access to Chinese AI models appears to misread the actual policy documents; the meetings were about preventing foreign acquisition of Chinese AI firms and IP outflow, not blocking model usage. The underlying Chinese document explicitly warns against over-regulating open-weight model exports.
AI Meets Cryptography 1: What AI Found in Cloudflare's Circl
zkSecurity's AI audit pipeline confirmed seven real bugs in Cloudflare's post-quantum cryptography library (including a critical float64 precision loss and a complete access-control break in attribute-based encryption), all now fixed upstream. Critically, AI produced candidate findings while humans validated exploitability — and the AI's own severity ratings were frequently wrong relative to confirmed severity, a useful calibration data point.

Engineering & AI Workflows

We charge $10k a week to delete AI-generated code
A consulting shop charging $10k/week to remediate bloated AI-generated codebases (e.g. 100k lines → 35k, same functionality) is now apparently a viable business. Their deliverables include a CLAUDE.md, lint rules, and CI checks to slow entropy accumulation — worth borrowing that "guardrails on re-entry" pattern even if you're not their customer.
Automating AI Away
A practical account of sandwiching LLM non-determinism between deterministic tools: fast formal tools on one side, strict workflow definitions on the other. Key insight: when Claude repeats a mistake, automate that step away; when it fails verification, automate the check. The author keeps instructions like "DO NOT PARSE ANYTHING MANUALLY, EVER" in caps and still needs periodic sweeps to remove violations.
Lilian Weng summarizes 35 papers on Harness Engineering for RSI
Lilian Weng's new research recap reframes recursive self-improvement around harness design rather than weight modification, arguing that specifying goals and context in the harness will remain necessary even as models improve. Also notable: Claude Cowork is now on mobile/web as a background task-running UI, and Codex Mobile iOS added task management, filtered diffs, and branch comparison.
I tested Anthropic's new Jacobian Lens on open models, then it turned into a local-model hallucination router
Using internal model workspace trajectory features (entropy slope, layer agreement) as a risk signal for confident-but-wrong answers, a small logistic regression router achieved AUC 0.787 on Gemma E4B versus 0.711 for logprob alone. Practical implication: "answer locally, escalate to cloud when workspace is foggy" is a real, lightweight pattern for reducing hallucination costs.
Most LLM Conformity Needs No Speaker: Measuring the Speaker-Free Floor in Peer-Pressure Benchmarks
Across six LLMs and seven datasets, simply repeating a wrong answer (without attributing it to anyone) caused models to flip from correct to wrong in 66.5% of cases. Direct relevance: prompts that include prior AI-generated answers or error examples risk contaminating model reasoning even without framing them as authoritative sources.
