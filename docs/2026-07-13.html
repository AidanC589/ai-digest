Engineering & AI Workflows

Migrating a production AI agent to GPT-5.6: 2.2x faster, 27% cheaper
Ploy's real-world migration from Claude Opus 4.8 to GPT-5.6 Sol shows 2.2× speed and 27% cost gains, but required fixing eval harnesses, tool schemas, caching assumptions, and reasoning replay — key lesson: your eval suite is secretly tuned to your incumbent model, not the task itself.
Fable gets another bump
Anthropic extended Claude Fable 5 access to July 19 on paid plans (50% of weekly limit), while OpenAI removed GPT-5.6 usage caps entirely for Plus/Business/Pro — the access uncertainty is a real operational risk if you're building Claude-dependent workflows.
Stop Telling Me to Ask an LLM
A pointed reminder that LLMs hit a ceiling on questions requiring genuine lived judgment — relevant for calibrating when to prompt harder vs. when the loop needs a human with scar tissue in it.
Directly Responsible Individuals (DRI)
Simon Willison argues agents should never be the DRI for a project because machines can't take accountability; useful framing for setting human review boundaries in AI-assisted dev workflows.
Why do people keep fine-tuning on summarized/censored SOTA CoT traces?
Anthropic's visible reasoning traces are not the actual chain-of-thought; fine-tuning on them (including popular Fable fine-tunes) is likely to degrade rather than improve model quality — directly relevant if you're evaluating distilled models for code tasks.
