General AI Developments

KPMG pulls report on AI usage due to apparent hallucinations
KPMG used AI to write a report about AI, resulting in fabricated claims about UBS, the NHS, and others that those organisations flatly denied; EY had a similar incident last month with fake footnotes. The pattern signals that AI-assisted document production without rigorous human verification is becoming a reputational liability.

Engineering & AI Workflows

The Coin Flip Judge? Reliability and Bias in LLM-as-a-Judge Evaluation
Rigorous study across 29 tasks finds pairwise LLM judge preferences flip 13.6% of the time (one question hit 56%), GPT-4o-mini shows 72% first-position bias, and cross-judge agreement is only 76%. If you're using LLM-as-a-Judge to evaluate AI-generated code quality, single-trial evaluation is too noisy — the paper recommends 11+ repeated trials with position randomization as minimum practice.
Why AI hasn't replaced software engineers, and won't
Narayanan and Kapoor's analysis identifies the three real bottlenecks AI can't automate away: deciding what to build, being accountable for what ships, and deep contextual understanding of codebase and business. Directly validates the "steering AI output" role over "writing code" — your job is the hard part.
Benchmarking Web Agent Safety under E-commerce Deceptive Interfaces
New benchmark shows current web agents are highly susceptible to deceptive UI patterns (fake ads, domain redirects, shopping manipulation), and prompt-based safety constraints frequently fail. Relevant if you're deploying or reviewing any agent code that interacts with external web interfaces.
AI is code – and can't be prompted into being smarter
The Register argues that prompt engineering has hard ceilings — models will confidently swallow and reproduce whatever you feed them regardless of how cleverly you ask. A useful counterweight to over-relying on prompt tricks as a substitute for model capability or human review.
Ponytail – make your AI agent think like the laziest senior dev in the room
Small tool/prompting framework designed to bias AI agents toward minimal, low-risk solutions rather than over-engineering — potentially useful for keeping AI-generated code from accruing unnecessary complexity.
