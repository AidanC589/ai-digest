## General AI Developments
- [Formalizing Latent Thoughts: Four Axioms of Thought Representation in LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27378) — A new axiomatic evaluation framework auditing 23 reasoning tasks found that current LLMs cannot reliably distinguish between two different questions *within the same task type*, and that their internal representations encode little beyond what's already in the input embedding — a structural flaw consistent across dense, distilled, and RL-trained models. Relevant signal for anyone trusting AI reasoning on nuanced code tasks.

## Engineering & AI Workflows
- [Quoting Jon Udell](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/28/jon-udell/#atom-everything) — Jon Udell's reframe is worth internalising: instead of "human in the loop," think of it as *your* loop, and you're recruiting agents into it. The practical implication is that agentic PRs should always be reviewable and attributable — if an agent produces an opaque black-box output, that's a process failure, not a feature.
