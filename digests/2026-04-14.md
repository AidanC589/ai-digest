## TL;DR
A real-world "vibe coding" incident exposed unencrypted medical patient data to the open internet — a stark reminder that AI-generated code without informed review is a liability, not a feature. ## General AI Developments
- [The AI revolution in math has arrived](https://www.quantamagazine.org/the-ai-revolution-in-math-has-arrived-20260413/) — Mathematicians including Terence Tao report that 2025–2026 marked a genuine inflection point: AI (including Claude and Gemini) is now helping produce journal-quality proofs and discover new results, with some conjecture-to-proof pipelines running with minimal human intervention. Relevant for anyone thinking about AI's ceiling on reasoning-heavy tasks.
- [Stanford report highlights growing disconnect between AI insiders and everyone else](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/13/stanford-report-highlights-growing-disconnect-between-ai-insiders-and-everyone-else/) — Stanford's annual AI index finds only 10% of Americans are more excited than concerned about AI, versus 56% of experts who expect net-positive impact — a gap that's widening and now generating real-world hostility toward AI leaders.

## Engineering & AI Workflows
- [An AI Vibe Coding Horror Story](https://www.tobhr.ch/an-ai-vibe-coding-horror-story/) — A medical practice built a patient management system with an AI coding agent, published it with zero backend access control (all auth logic in client-side JS), unencrypted data on a US server without a DPA, and voice recordings sent to AI APIs without patient consent. A direct case study in why AI-generated code requires a reviewer who understands what was built — not just that it runs.
- [The human cost of 10x: How AI is physically breaking senior engineers](https://techtrenches.dev/p/the-human-cost-of-10x-how-ai-is-physically) — UC Berkeley research embedded in a 200-person company found AI doesn't reduce work, it intensifies it via task expansion, blurred work boundaries, and implicit pressure. Key stat: 88% burnout rate among the highest AI-productivity workers, who are also twice as likely to quit. PR review quality drops from 87% defect detection under 100 lines to 28% over 1,000 lines — and AI is driving PR volume up 98%.
- [TUI to see where Claude Code tokens actually go](https://www.reddit.com/r/ClaudeAI/comments/1skqub5/tui_to_see_where_claude_code_tokens_actually_go/) — A developer spending $200+/day on Claude Code built `codeburn` (npx codeburn), which parses session transcripts from `~/.claude/projects/` and classifies spend by task type without any LLM calls. Finding: 56% of spend was pure conversation turns with no tool use; actual file edits were only 21%. Useful for identifying where your prompts are inefficient.
- [Steve Yegge (via Simon Willison)](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/13/steve-yegge/#atom-everything) — A viral claim that Google's internal AI adoption mirrors John Deere's was publicly disputed by Addy Osmani (40K+ Googlers using agentic coding weekly) and Demis Hassabis. The underlying pattern — 20% agentic power users, 60% still on chat-only tools, 20% refusers — is reportedly consistent across most of the industry regardless of which side is right about Google.

## Try This
Run `npx codeburn` against your Claude Code session history this week and look at your task-type breakdown. If — like the tool's author — you find more than half your spend is in "conversation" turns with no tool use, that's a signal your prompts are generating dialogue where they should be generating action. Rewrite your high-cost prompt patterns to front-load constraints and expected output format, so Claude moves directly to tool use rather than confirming intent first. The `~/.claude/projects/` transcripts are already there; no configuration needed.
