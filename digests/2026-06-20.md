# AI Digest — 2026-06-20

## TL;DR
Open-weight models are rapidly closing the capability gap with closed APIs, making the cost case for proprietary models increasingly hard to justify. Meanwhile, a Nature study is raising red flags about AI eroding skills — worth understanding if you're steering AI-generated code all day.

## General AI Developments
- [Is AI ruining our skills? Early results are in – and they're not good](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01947-1) — Early research published in Nature suggests AI assistance is degrading human skills over time; directly relevant if your entire workflow is reviewing and steering AI output rather than building the underlying knowledge yourself.
- [The economics of AI are starting to favor open models](https://www.reddit.com/r/LocalLLaMA/comments/1ua5b16/the_economics_of_ai_are_starting_to_favor_open/) — The upper-left quadrant of capability-vs-cost is now dominated by open-weight models (DeepSeek, Qwen, GLM, Kimi); for most coding workloads the frontier gap is shrinking faster than the price gap, which has direct implications for team AI tooling decisions.

## Engineering & AI Workflows
