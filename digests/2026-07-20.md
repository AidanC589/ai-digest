## General AI Developments
- [AI advice made people less accurate but more confident – study](https://thenextweb.com/news/ai-advice-suppresses-critical-thinking-wrong-answers-study) — Researchers found AI availability collapsed willingness to say "I don't know" from 44% to 3%, dropped accuracy to one-third of baseline, while confidence nearly tripled. Directly relevant to AI code review: if you're using AI to audit AI-generated code, you may be systematically more wrong and less aware of it.
- [HuggingFace security incident report: "the attacker was bound by no usage policy, while our own forensic work was blocked by the guardrails"](https://www.reddit.com/r/LocalLLaMA/comments/1v0ywoi/huggingface_security_incident_report_the_attacker/) — HuggingFace was hit by an autonomous AI agent attack; their own forensic AI analysis was blocked by commercial API safety guardrails when processing real exploit payloads, forcing them to run open-weight models locally. Key security finding: commercial frontier models are unsuitable for incident response on real attack data.

## Engineering & AI Workflows
- [AI advice made people less accurate but more confident – study](https://thenextweb.com/news/ai-advice-suppresses-critical-thinking-wrong-answers-study) — The "cognitive surrender" pattern (accepting wrong AI answers 80% of the time with higher confidence) has direct workflow implications: structured skepticism and explicit "I don't know" checkpoints need to be deliberately built into AI-assisted review processes, since the tools themselves won't prompt you to doubt them.
- [HuggingFace security incident report: "the attacker was bound by no usage policy, while our own forensic work was blocked by the guardrails"](https://www.reddit.com/r/LocalLLaMA/comments/1v0ywoi/huggingface_security_incident_report_the_attacker/) — Running security-sensitive workflows — including AI-assisted code audits involving malicious samples or secrets — through commercial APIs exposes data and may be blocked mid-analysis; local open-weight models are the practical solution for any sensitive forensic or security review pipeline.
- [SkillCorpus: Consolidating and Evaluating the Open Skill Ecosystem for Real-World LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15557) — SKILL.md files (reusable procedural knowledge packaged for agents) consistently improved benchmark performance (+7.5pp on SkillsBench), suggesting that maintaining a curated personal skill library for your coding agents is worth the investment — the gains are real but bounded by coverage of your specific task types.

## Try This
To counteract cognitive surrender in your own review workflow, add a mandatory "uncertainty pass" prompt at the end of any AI code review session: after receiving AI feedback on a PR, send a follow-up asking *"Which of your suggestions above are you least confident about, and what would you need to verify them?"* This forces the model to surface its own uncertainty rather than presenting everything with equal authority — and it primes you to apply more scrutiny to the shakiest claims before approving.

## Token Saving Techniques
- [VarRate: Training-Free Variable-Rate KV Cache Compression for Long-Context LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15498) — VarRate assigns variable low-rank budgets to KV cache tokens by query salience (no training required), staying within 0.8 points of uncompressed model quality at 20% memory budget on LongBench — a practical option for long-context inference cost reduction without model retraining.
- [sponsors/tirth8205](https://github.com/sponsors/tirth8205) — The local code intelligence graph approach (building a persistent codebase map) is explicitly designed to reduce context sent to AI tools on reviews and large-repo workflows, cutting tokens by feeding only relevant code rather than whole files.
